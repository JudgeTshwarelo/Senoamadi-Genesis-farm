Senoamadi Poultry Industry Co-operative Limited is about to be registered. Co-operatives like SPI are typically involved in various agricultural activities—potentially including poultry production or support services—contributing to local economic development within Kgomoschool.

Senoamadi Poultry Industry Context

The Senoamadi Poultry Industry is a minor agricultural sector valued at around R10 000, currently not employing people. It aims to be the pillar of food security, providing affordable protein to millions while being key for jobs and economic inclusion, especially as a black-owned and emerging enterprise through contract farming and transformation initiatives.

Developments and Support

SPI calls on government and poultry partnerships to uplift small-scale black-owned enterprises by improving access to inputs (like feed and chicks) and financing mechanisms. Investment in hatchery expansions, abattoirs, processing facilities, and farm upgrades can create significant employment opportunities.

Trade, Competition, and Challenges Outlook

The sector faces challenges including:

  • Feed costs and input price volatility, especially for hatchery.
  • Disease threats such as avian influenza outbreaks.
  • Competition from imports, especially low-cost mechanically deboned meat.
  • Regulatory and biosecurity barriers affecting exports and vaccination programs.

Despite challenges, SPI continues investing in expanding production capacity, promoting export growth—especially to regional markets like SADC and AfCFTA—and increasing black participation in ownership and farming.

Summary

Senoamadi Poultry Industries is positioned within a growing yet challenged South African poultry ecosystem that strives for inclusive growth, job creation, and supply stability. Cooperative and contract farming models supported by industry master plans aim to increase local production capacity and market access for emerging and black-owned farmers, fostering sustainable development in Limpopo and beyond.
